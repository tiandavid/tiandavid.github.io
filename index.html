Host seeking behavior in Aedes aegypti

Aedes aegypti mosquitoes are potent disease vectors that have evolved a robust preference for humans as hosts. Thanks to advances in CRISPR trangenics, we are able to investigate the genetic and neural basis of this fasincating behavior.

Genetic basis of color in Heliconius

Heliconius butterflies exhibit striking diversity in wing patterns and have served as an excellent system for studying how morphology evolves and develops. Recent advances in genomics and genetic techniques now allow us to investigate the genetic basis for how biodiversity is generated.
